Curriculum Vitae

Andreas Thom

I am a Professor of Geometry at TU Dresden, Fakultät für Mathematik. My research focus is mainly on geometric group theory, real algebraic geometry, algebraic topology, ergodic theory, and operator algebras. And I enjoy communicating mathematics to a broader audience.

Academic timeline

  • 2000 Certificate of Advanced Study in Mathematics, University of Cambridge.
  • 2003 PhD (Promotion) from the University of Münster under Joachim Cuntz.
  • 2003-2005 Postdoctoral researcher at the University of Münster.
  • 2005-2007 Postdoctoral researcher at the University of Göttingen.
  • 2007-2009 Junior Professor (W1) of Geometrical Aspects of Pure Mathematics, University of Göttingen.
  • 2009-2014 Professor (W3) of Theoretical Mathematics, University of Leipzig.
  • 2014-present Professor (W3) of Geometry, TU Dresden.

Awards & Honors, and Service

  • 2011 ERC Starting Grant ("Geometry and Analysis of Group Rings").
  • 2016 ERC Consolidator Grant ("Groups, Dynamics, and Approximation").
  • 2018 Invited speaker (Topology / Analysis & Operator Algebras) at ICM Rio de Janeiro.
  • Since 2013 Member of the Max Planck Society and External Scientific Member of the Max Planck Institute for Mathematics in the Sciences.
  • Since 2016 Member of the Scientific Advisory Board of the Mathematisches Forschungsinstitut Oberwolfach and the Gesellschaft für Mathematische Forschung.
  • Since 2017 Scientific director of Erlebnisland Mathematik.
